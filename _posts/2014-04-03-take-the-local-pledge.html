Take the LOCAL PLEDGE!

Yesterday, I spent a worthwhile 3 hours away from my farm to attend a class for farmer's market managers from all over Indiana. One of the speakers talked about My Local Indiana, a grass-roots, volunteer-run initiative that is working to connect Indiana farmers to each other and to the food-buying public, which is everyone of us.

One of the sad truths about Indiana food economics is that we export most of the food we produce here, and most of what we eat comes in from other states and even other countries. It's not hard to see that something's wrong with this picture. By shopping at farmer's markets, you're already helping keep food dollars in Indiana by supporting local farmers and food producers.

According to My Local Indiana, if each household substituted just 10% of their current food purchases with local food, we would keep $1.7 billion food dollars from leaving our state.Based on estimates of average household food purchases, that 10% amounts to about $680 per year per household, or only about $13 per week! We know that many of you already purchase much more than that at our market, and we are grateful.

Now you can take the next step and sign up for the 10% Pledge. It's easy-log on to mylocalindiana.com and answer a couple of questions to register. Every week, you'll get a short e-mail questionnaire about your food purchases. It helps you track the portion of your food dollar that stays local, and reminds you of the difference you're making for local farmers and food producers.

See you in the hoop house on Saturdays from 9 to 12:30 through April!

  
~Market Master Maria Smietana
