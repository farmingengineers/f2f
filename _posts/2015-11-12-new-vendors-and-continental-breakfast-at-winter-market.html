New Vendors and Continental Breakfast at Winter Market
Winter market will be held in the heated building near the corn cribs. See you Saturday mornings 9 - 12:30 Many thanks to everyone who came out last Saturday for the first market of the winter season. Hard to believe that we already completed our third summer at the Normandy Farms location, and are starting our third winter! If you haven't already done so, stop and say hello to our newest vendors. Alan and Amy McKamey own Heritage Meadows Farm in Hendricks County, and will supply us with pork, poultry, homemade soaps, jams/jellies, and other products from their farm. Nin Sweets joins us as a guest vendor to try the market out and see if they want to become permanent. Charlee and Shellie's honey-and-lemon-based products can be used as marinades, dressings, spreads, and even as a remedy for sore throats and cold symptoms. Finally, thanks to your generous donations to our ongoing GoFundMe campaign we were able to buy our Marion County Food Vendor Permit, which allows the market to serve simple prepared foods. We'll be selling combos that consist of hot English muffins with spreads and a choice of juices and coffee. Just follow your nose to the food table! See you Saturday from 9-12:30 in the heated clubhouse building just south of the Granary.

Blue Yonder Organic Farm A 43-acre diversified farm committed to producing healthy organic food, produced exclusively by military veterans. The farm produces a wide variety of fresh vegetables, fruits, meats, eggs, and honey using organic methods. These products are sold under the label of Homegrown by Heroes, a national certification designating agricultural products grown by military veterans. Blue Yonder Organic Farm will have chicken and eggs at market, plus whole duck and duck breasts. Countryside Orchard We will have Suncrisp, Rubinette, Enterprise, & Fuji apples. Also Brussels sprouts, green peppers, cabbage, carrots & beets. Taking orders for holiday gift boxes. Looking forward to seeing everyone! Stan & Judy
